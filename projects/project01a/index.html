Hallucinations

A Personal Examination

Overview

Hallucinations is a shot on video movie about three brothers left home alone while their mom is stuck working through the night. After one brother meets the nextdoor monk, the three begin to experience increasingly violent and deadly hallucinations. The film stars John Polonia, Mark Polonia, and Todd Michael Smith. The three of them are also the directors, cinematographers, etc...
Shot on video (SOV) films are films shot directly on consumer available camcorders. During the 80's, many artists utilized these goods to film their own movies and distribute them to as many video stores as possible. Essentially, homemade movies were able to be mass produced and consumed by fellow common people. Horror was essentially the only genre prominent in this movement, as tons of creatives would film their own shock/horror films at home with no budget.

The lack of a budget or acting ability is a prominent part of this film. Props are (in a way, charmingly) handmade, imperfect things that are constructed out of household materials. It's impossible to have a scene with all three brothers, as one always has to be filming/directing. Any gore is instantly cut away and returned to to avoid the need for mechanical effects.
Despite this, there's a clear passion. Scenes where they try and portray throwing up organs (a pink, tied up t-shirt) or a bizarre worm creature stalks the shower (an old ribbed pipe with some clay at the end). It's easy to have your nonexistent budget control your scene choices, but that doesn't happen here.

Review

Objectively, this is a terrible film. The dialogue is hard to understand, the acting is horrible, the props are cheap... And really, not much happens beyond continually weird hallucinations from the brothers. Characters are nonexistent beyond the defining trait of one brother being adopted and another being mentally handicapped, and I can't remember a single name. There are some wins, though.
A film where passion is clearly put into it is more enjoyable than another lifeless straight to DVD piece of garbage. Even if scenes end up being corny, it's enjoyable to see three fans of horror create something they clearly care about. The music is also quite effective in being eerie. Scenes where things would be objectively funny (a shaking clay human that looks nothing lifelike) end up so bizarre that you get uncomfortable among music made from slowed down growing and dog whining.
